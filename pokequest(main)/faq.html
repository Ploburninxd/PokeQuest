❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about PokéQuest

🎮 Getting Started
How do I start a new game?
Click New Game on the main menu. You'll be asked to choose one of three starter Pokémon — Bulbasaur (Grass), Charmander (Fire), or Squirtle (Water). Your starter choice affects your early battles, so pick one that suits your style! Once chosen, click Explore to begin your adventure.
Does my starter count towards the Pokédex?
No — your starter Pokémon is given to you as a gift and does not count towards your Pokédex. Only Pokémon you catch in the wild are recorded. This is intentional so the "First Catch!" achievement feels earned.
How do I save and load my progress?
Click 💾 Save to download a .json save file to your device. To continue later, click 📂 Load and select your save file. Your game also auto-saves to your browser every 60 seconds — but always use the file save before closing the tab, as browser storage can be cleared.
💡 Tip: Name your save files clearly so you can find them easily!
⚔️ Battles & Catching
How do I catch a Pokémon?
When a wild Pokémon appears, select your fighter then use the ⚔️ Move button to attack it. Lower its HP to increase your catch chance, then throw a Pokéball from the battle screen. The catch rates are: Poké Ball 30%, Great Ball 50%, Ultra Ball 70%, Master Ball 100%. Getting the enemy below 10% HP gives a massive catch bonus!
💡 Tip: Watch the "Catch: X%" display on the enemy card — it updates in real time.
What does type effectiveness mean?
Each Pokémon has a type (Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, etc.) and moves deal extra or reduced damage depending on the target's type. The move selection screen shows a badge on every move: ⚡ Super Effective (×2), ▲ Effective (×1.5), — Normal, ▼ Not Very Effective (×0.5), or ✗ No Effect (×0). Always choose super effective moves when you can!
My party is full — what happens when I catch a new Pokémon?
Your party holds up to 6 Pokémon. If you catch one while your party is full, it is automatically sent to the 🖥️ PC Box. You'll see a message saying "Caught X! Party full — sent to PC." Use the PC Box button to manage your stored Pokémon and swap them into your party.
Can I switch Pokémon during battle?
Yes — click the 🔄 Switch button in battle to choose a different Pokémon from your party. Fainted Pokémon (0 HP) cannot be selected. Switching doesn't use your turn, but the enemy still attacks after you switch.
📈 Progression
How do Pokémon level up and evolve?
Your Pokémon gain XP from winning battles and catching wild Pokémon. When they accumulate enough XP (level × 50), they level up automatically — gaining higher HP and Attack. At certain levels they evolve into stronger forms, gaining better stats and a new appearance. You can also use a 🍬 Rare Candy from the shop to instantly level up any Pokémon.
How do I unlock new regions?
Regions unlock automatically when your strongest Pokémon reaches the required level. The order is: Kanto Forest (Lv.1) → Mountains (Lv.21) → Coast (Lv.36) → City (Lv.46) → Plains (Lv.48) → Fuchsia (Lv.52) → Volcano (Lv.56) → Lavender Tower (Lv.66) → Safari Zone (Lv.76) → Victory Road (Lv.86). A notification appears when a new region unlocks.
How do TMs work?
TMs (Technical Machines) teach powerful moves to your Pokémon. Buy them from the 🏪 Shop under the TMs tab. To use one, go to your Pokémon's detail page (tap any Pokémon in your party list) and press 📀 Use TM. If your Pokémon already has 4 moves, you'll be asked which move to replace. TMs are not consumed — you keep them after teaching.
🏆 Gyms & Achievements
How do gym battles work?
Each region (except Kanto Forest and Safari Zone) has a Gym Leader. Once you unlock a region, press the 🏆 Gym button to challenge them. Select your Pokémon, then use the Move button to pick attacks — just like wild battles. Beating the leader earns a badge and credits. There are 8 gyms total: Brock, Misty, Lt. Surge, Erika, Koga, Blaine, Sabrina, and Champion Lance.
How do I unlock achievements?
Achievements unlock automatically as you play — catching Pokémon, winning battles, earning badges, evolving Pokémon, and more. When one unlocks, a toast notification appears in the top-right corner. View all 18 achievements and your progress by clicking the 🏆 Achievements button.
What is the Buddy System?
Open a Pokémon's detail page and tap 🤝 Make Buddy to set it as your walking partner. As you explore, your buddy gains happiness. Higher happiness unlocks bonuses like bonus items and battle boosts. You can see your buddy's happiness bar in the sidebar.
